What proved the best delusion of
the season was perpetrated upon the
public, by a nine year old lad of the
race Tuesday afternoon. Over two
hours people stood in the streets and
back yards looking skyward at the
“airship" supposed to be from Luna
park. In fact it was so high above
the ground that it did not get wet
during the downpour of rain about 4
o'clock. It could be seen with the
naked eye to dip and plunge, and with
the opera glasses, the American flag
could be seen floating above the "bal
loon.” About dusk the “airship” be
gan to settle rapidly in the neighbor
hood of Woodland avenue and May
flower street to which point nearly a
thousand people rushed. It appeared
to be of enormous size, but when it
reached earth was nothing but a red,
white and blue tissue paper kite, of
the tailess, triangular shape about
two feet long on the side, and four
yards of red, white and blue cheese
cloth attached, giving it the flag ap
pearance. Common white twine was
used in sailing the kite and at times
even the boy lost track of his kite.
