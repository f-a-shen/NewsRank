His Talk Has the Right Ring—A
Leya! Member of the Race.
Cincinnati O. —The local Daily Tri
bune of the 18th contained the fol
lowing:
Prof. W. P. Dabney, one of ihe lead
ing colored citizens of this county,
owner of Dabney building on McAl
lister street, the founder of the fa
mous Douglass league and the most
prominent independent republican
here for many years; also the first
colored paymaster of this city, was
interviewed yesterday relative to his
attitude in the coming campaign. He
stated frankly and unequivocably his
intention of supporting the straight
republican ticket, and doing every
thing possible for its success.
His resolution in that regard was
caused by no personal grievance,
since no position or benefit had been
sought by him from the democratic
party; but while in the past he had
not coincided with many actions of
the republican party, yet the present
administration of the democratic
party has proven to be a calamity to
the Negroes of this community.
Its record has been signalized by
race prejudice of the rankest sort.
Not satisfied with dropping from cler
ical positions all colored men and re
placing them by whites, the board
went a step further and publicly sup
ported its members who declared they
would vote for no Negro as sanitary
officer.
The position sought by the colored
man in that case was menial, both in
character and compensation. After
such an exhibition, very few colored
men, when informed of the true condi
tion of affairs will be found aiding an
administration whose actions have
been so conspicuously detrimental to
their interests.
