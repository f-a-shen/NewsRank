The Chorus Struck.
New York. Jan. 4.—Gounod’s Faust
was sung at the Metropolitan opera
house last nigh* without a chorus, the
result of a strike of the recently or
ganized Chorus union. The union, on
Monday, demanded that salaries be
raised nearly 50 per cent. Director
Conreid met the representatives of the
union and told them that if members
of the chorus would come to him as in
dividuals he would try to better their
condition, but would refuse to treat
with them as members of a labor
union. Last night none of the chorus
put in an appearance. The chorus
numbered 150 persons.
