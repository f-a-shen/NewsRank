Civil War Tragedy Recalled.
A tragedy of the Civil war was
vividly brought to mind recently at
Centralia where the body of Zadoc
Bryson, killed in 1863, was disintered.
A pocket comb with the dead man's
name upon it was found in perfect
condition. Lead pencils in his pocket
were still serviceable. A small bot
tle of medicine in his pocket was se
curely corked and two worms were
inhabiting the liquid. His boots were
in perfect condition and in one of
them was found a bowie knife that
even the vigilant eyes of the Federal
soldiers had failed to find. The most
perfect thing in the grave was his
coat. After all these years it was
still strong enough to lift what re
mained of his body. In the back and
side could plainly be seen the numer
ous- bullet holes where the death mis
sies entered his body. A monument
is to be erected to the dead man’s
memory by a son.
